# Active vs. Passive Candidate Messaging

## Overview

The job market consists of two fundamentally different audiences:
- **Active candidates**: Currently searching for a new role
- **Passive candidates**: Employed and not actively looking, but potentially open

Effective recruitment advertising requires tailored approaches for each audience, as their motivations, behaviors, and messaging receptivity differ significantly.

---

## Active Job Seekers

### Profile

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Status | Unemployed, underemployed, or actively seeking change |
| Intent | High - actively searching job boards and career pages |
| Timeline | Often urgent (weeks to 1-2 months) |
| Volume | Applying to multiple roles simultaneously |
| Platform behavior | Job boards, career pages, Google search |

### Motivations

1. **Immediate need** - May be unemployed or facing layoff
2. **Escape** - Unhappy in current role, toxic environment
3. **Career change** - Seeking new direction
4. **Growth blockage** - Hit ceiling at current company
5. **Compensation gap** - Significantly underpaid

### Messaging Strategy

**Lead with**: Job specifics, clear requirements, and quick application process

| Emphasis | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Job clarity | They're evaluating many options quickly |
| Easy apply | Reduce friction for high-volume applicants |
| Timeline | When will they hear back? |
| Requirements | Can they self-qualify? |
| Compensation | Major decision factor, especially if unemployed |

### Effective Language

- "Apply now" (direct CTA)
- "Immediate opening"
- "Hiring this week"
- "Competitive salary: $X-$Y"
- "No [credential] required" (when applicable)
- "Quick application process"
- "Hear back within [timeframe]"

### Avoid

- Lengthy brand storytelling (they need specifics)
- Vague job descriptions
- Complex application processes
- "Rock star" or exclusive language
- Excessive requirements

### Optimal Platforms

| Platform | Why Effective |
|----------|---------------|
| Indeed | Primary job search destination |
| Google Ads | High-intent keyword targeting |
| LinkedIn Jobs | Professional job seekers |
| Company careers page | Direct applicants |

### Ad Format Recommendations

- **Google RSAs**: Clear job title, salary, location, "Apply Now"
- **Indeed**: Straightforward posting, Easy Apply enabled
- **LinkedIn**: Job slots with clear requirements

## Passive Candidates

### Profile

| Characteristic | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Status | Currently employed, not actively searching |
| Intent | Low to moderate - open to right opportunity |
| Timeline | Longer decision cycle (months) |
| Volume | Selective, few applications if any |
| Platform behavior | LinkedIn networking, industry content, social media |

### Motivations

1. **Opportunity** - Something significantly better than current role
2. **Recognition** - Feeling undervalued or overlooked
3. **Mission** - Company/product they believe in
4. **People** - Respected leaders, great team
5. **Growth** - Unlocking new trajectory
6. **Compensation** - Meaningful increase (20%+ typical threshold)

### Messaging Strategy

**Lead with**: Opportunity, company story, and "what's in it for you"

| Emphasis | Why |
|----------|-----|
| Company/mission | Must compel them to consider leaving security |
| Opportunity narrative | What makes this worth the risk? |
| Social proof | Leaders, team, company trajectory |
| Exclusive feel | "We're reaching out to you specifically" |
| Low commitment ask | "Learn more" not "Apply now" |

### Effective Language

- "Imagine if..." (paint the picture)
- "Join the team that [achievement]"
- "Opportunity to [unique benefit]"
- "Work alongside [respected name/team]"
- "Be part of [mission/transformation]"
- "Curious? Let's talk."
- "Confidential conversation"

### Avoid

- "Apply now" (too much commitment)
- Job posting format (feels mass-marketed)
- Lengthy requirements lists
- Transactional language
- Pressure tactics

### Optimal Platforms

| Platform | Why Effective |
|----------|---------------|
| LinkedIn InMail | Direct, personal outreach |
| LinkedIn Sponsored Content | Brand awareness in feed |
| Meta (FB/IG) | Lifestyle/culture content |
| Programmatic display | Retargeting career page visitors |

### Ad Format Recommendations

- **LinkedIn InMail**: Personalized, from real recruiter
- **LinkedIn Sponsored Content**: Culture videos, employee stories
- **Meta**: Behind-the-scenes, day-in-the-life content
- **Retargeting**: "Still thinking about joining us?"

## Messaging Comparison

| Element | Active | Passive |
|---------|--------|---------|
| CTA | "Apply Now" | "Learn More" / "Let's Talk" |
| Tone | Direct, efficient | Aspirational, personal |
| Lead | Job specifics | Company/opportunity story |
| Compensation | State upfront | Imply competitiveness |
| Length | Concise | Can be longer/narrative |
| Urgency | "Hiring now" | "When you're ready" |
| Format | Job listing | Story/content |

## Hybrid Approach: "Open to Work"

LinkedIn's "Open to Work" signals create a middle category.

### Characteristics
- Currently employed but signaling openness
- Higher intent than passive, less urgent than active
- Receptive to direct outreach
- Often have specific criteria in mind

### Messaging Approach
- Acknowledge their openness directly
- Present opportunity fit
- Respect their current position
- Offer confidential conversation

## Funnel Considerations

### Active Candidate Funnel
```
Job Search → Click Ad → View Posting → Apply → Screen → Interview
(Optimize for: Conversion rate, time to apply)
```

### Passive Candidate Funnel
```
Content Exposure → Interest → Research → Outreach → Conversation → Consider → Apply
(Optimize for: Awareness, engagement, relationship)
```

## Budget Allocation Guidance

[VERIFY] Optimal budget split varies by role type, seniority, and market conditions.

| Role Type | Active % | Passive % |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Entry-level | 80% | 20% |
| Mid-level | 60% | 40% |
| Senior | 40% | 60% |
| Executive | 20% | 80% |

**Rationale**: Higher seniority = lower active job seeker volume, greater passive recruitment ROI.
