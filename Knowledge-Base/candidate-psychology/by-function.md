# Candidate Messaging by Job Function

## Overview

Different professional functions have distinct cultures, values, and decision-making criteria. This guide provides messaging frameworks for Aquent's primary talent categories: Creative, Technical, and Corporate functions.

---

## Creative Functions

### Includes
- Graphic Designers
- UX/UI Designers
- Art Directors
- Copywriters
- Video/Motion Designers
- Brand Designers
- Creative Directors
- Content Creators

### Primary Motivations

| Motivation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Portfolio work | Quality projects they can showcase |
| Creative freedom | Autonomy to solve problems their way |
| Brand prestige | Working on recognized brands/products |
| Tools & resources | Access to latest design tools |
| Peer quality | Collaborating with other talented creatives |
| Work environment | Aesthetic, inspiring workspace (physical or remote) |

### Messaging Priorities

1. **Show, don't tell** - Creative candidates respond to visual proof
2. **Highlight the work** - What brands/projects will they work on?
3. **Emphasize creative autonomy** - Not just executing orders
4. **Mention tools/tech** - Figma, Adobe CC, latest design systems
5. **Culture over corporate** - Avoid overly formal language

### Effective Language

- "Craft compelling [work type]"
- "Creative ownership from concept to delivery"
- "Work on brands like [names]"
- "Collaborative creative team"
- "Latest tools and technology"
- "Build your portfolio with [type of work]"
- "Design systems you'll be proud of"

### Avoid

- "Execute designs per specifications"
- Overly corporate/formal tone
- Excessive process/bureaucracy emphasis
- "Production artist" when role has creative scope
- Wall-of-text job descriptions

### Platform & Format Tips

| Platform | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | Showcase visual work, culture content |
| Meta | Video/carousel showing team's work |
| Google Ads | Keep copy creative, not corporate |
| Portfolio sites | Sponsor on Dribbble, Behance [VERIFY] |

### Visual Elements
- Use the team's actual work in ads
- Show workspace (studio, tools, team)
- Feature employee portfolios
- Behind-the-scenes creative process content

## Technical Functions

### Includes
- Software Engineers
- Data Scientists
- DevOps/SRE
- Product Managers (technical)
- QA Engineers
- Solutions Architects
- Technical Leads
- Engineering Managers

### Primary Motivations

| Motivation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Technical challenge | Complex, interesting problems |
| Tech stack | Modern, relevant technologies |
| Engineering culture | Code quality, best practices |
| Impact at scale | Systems serving many users |
| Autonomy | Ability to make technical decisions |
| Growth & learning | New technologies, mentorship |
| Compensation | Highly competitive market |

### Messaging Priorities

1. **Lead with tech stack** - Languages, frameworks, infrastructure
2. **Describe the challenge** - What problems will they solve?
3. **Engineering culture signals** - Code review, testing, CI/CD
4. **Team composition** - Who will they work with?
5. **Be specific, not vague** - Technical candidates filter quickly

### Effective Language

- "Build [specific technical challenge]"
- "Tech stack: [specific technologies]"
- "Scale systems to [X users/requests]"
- "Ship to production [frequency]"
- "Engineering-driven culture"
- "Full ownership of [system/product]"
- "Join a team of [X] engineers"
- "Remote-first with [collaboration style]"

### Avoid

- "Rockstar developer" / "Ninja coder"
- Vague descriptions ("cutting-edge technology")
- Non-technical managers writing technical specs
- Unrealistic requirement lists
- "Fast-paced startup environment" without specifics
- Buzzword overload without substance

### Platform & Format Tips

| Platform | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | Technical blog posts, engineering culture |
| Google Ads | Specific tech stack keywords |
| GitHub/Stack Overflow | Sponsored presence [VERIFY] |
| Hacker News | "Who's Hiring" threads |
| Dev.to/Medium | Engineering blog visibility |

### Technical Specificity

**Good**: "Python backend engineer - Django, PostgreSQL, AWS. Building ML pipeline serving 10M daily predictions."

**Bad**: "Full-stack developer needed for fast-paced tech startup. Looking for someone passionate about technology."

### Requirements Balance
[VERIFY] Studies on job requirement inflation's impact on application rates.

- List true requirements vs. nice-to-haves
- "5+ years Python" vs. "Experience with Python"
- Avoid degree requirements when not necessary
- Include "or equivalent experience" for self-taught

## Corporate Functions

### Includes
- Marketing
- Finance
- HR/People Operations
- Legal
- Operations
- Customer Success
- Sales
- Project Management
- Administrative

### Primary Motivations

| Motivation | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| Career progression | Clear path to next level |
| Company stability | Established, growing organization |
| Work-life balance | Predictable hours, flexibility |
| Benefits package | Health, 401k, PTO |
| Team dynamics | Supportive management |
| Company reputation | Brand recognition |
| Compensation | Competitive within function |

### Messaging Priorities

1. **Company brand/stability** - Who are they joining?
2. **Role clarity** - Clear scope and expectations
3. **Growth opportunity** - What's next after this role?
4. **Benefits & flexibility** - Total compensation picture
5. **Team/manager** - Who will they work with?

### Effective Language

- "Join [company]'s growing [function] team"
- "Clear path to [next role]"
- "Competitive salary plus comprehensive benefits"
- "Collaborative team environment"
- "Work-life balance and flexibility"
- "Supportive management"
- "Make an impact on [business outcome]"

### Avoid

- Overemphasis on "hustle" or long hours
- Vague growth claims without evidence
- Missing benefits/compensation information
- "Wear many hats" without role clarity
- Startup chaos marketed as a benefit

### Platform & Format Tips

| Platform | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | Company brand, employee testimonials |
| Indeed | Clear requirements, salary, benefits |
| Google Ads | Function-specific keywords |
| Meta | Company culture, team content |

### Segment-Specific Notes

**Marketing**: Emphasize brand, creative campaigns, tools (HubSpot, Marketo, etc.)

**Finance**: Stability, growth, specific systems (NetSuite, SAP, etc.)

**HR**: Company culture, people-first values, HRIS systems

**Operations**: Impact on efficiency, process improvement, scale

**Sales**: Commission structure, territory, quota attainability, tools

| Element | Creative | Technical | Corporate |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Lead with | The work | The challenge | The company |

| Compensation | Mention | Essential | Important |
