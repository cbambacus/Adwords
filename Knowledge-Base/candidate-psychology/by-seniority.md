# Candidate Messaging by Seniority Level

## Overview

Messaging must be tailored to the career stage, motivations, and decision factors that vary significantly across seniority levels. What attracts an entry-level candidate will not resonate with a senior executive.

---

## Entry-Level (0-2 Years Experience)

### Primary Motivations
- Learning and skill development
- Career foundation and growth path
- Job security and stability
- Mentorship opportunities
- Work-life balance

### Messaging Priorities

| Priority | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Training & development | Building skills for career advancement |
| Clear growth path | Visibility into promotion timeline |
| Company stability | Job security concerns |
| Team culture | Social/collaborative environment |
| Benefits basics | Health insurance, PTO |

### Effective Language
- "Launch your career"
- "Learn from industry experts"
- "Structured training program"
- "Clear path to promotion"
- "Collaborative team environment"
- "No experience required" (when true)

### Avoid
- Jargon or assumed industry knowledge
- Emphasis on "fast-paced" without growth context
- Senior-level responsibility descriptions
- Vague advancement claims

### Platform Considerations
- **Indeed**: Emphasize training, entry-level friendly
- **LinkedIn**: Highlight company brand, internship-to-hire path
- **Meta**: Video content showing team culture, day-in-the-life

## Mid-Level (3-7 Years Experience)

### Primary Motivations
- Career advancement opportunities
- Skill expansion into new areas
- Compensation competitiveness
- Work-life balance and flexibility
- Impact and ownership
- Company reputation for career trajectory

### Messaging Priorities

| Priority | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Growth opportunities | Moving toward senior roles |
| Challenging projects | Expanding portfolio and skills |
| Competitive compensation | Market-rate expectations established |
| Flexibility | Life stage often involves major commitments |
| Leadership exposure | Learning from and visibility to leaders |

### Effective Language
- "Take your career to the next level"
- "Lead projects from start to finish"
- "Expand your expertise"
- "Competitive salary and benefits"
- "Flexible work arrangements"
- "Cross-functional collaboration"
- "Direct impact on company growth"

### Avoid
- Entry-level positioning
- Overemphasis on "learning" without ownership
- Vague titles without clear level
- Downplaying compensation discussion

### Platform Considerations
- **LinkedIn**: Detailed role scope, career path visibility
- **Google Ads**: Target keywords like "[role] jobs" + location
- **Indeed**: Salary transparency critical at this level

---

## Senior-Level (8-15 Years Experience)

### Primary Motivations
- Strategic impact and influence
- Leadership opportunities
- Compensation and equity
- Company mission and values alignment
- Work-life integration
- Recognition and respect

### Messaging Priorities

| Priority | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Leadership scope | Managing people/functions |
| Strategic impact | Shaping company direction |
| Compensation package | Salary, bonus, equity expected |
| Company trajectory | Growth stage, market position |
| Team quality | Who they'll work with and lead |

### Effective Language
- "Shape the future of [function/company]"
- "Build and lead a team"
- "Drive strategic initiatives"
- "Seat at the table"
- "Competitive compensation including equity"
- "Work alongside industry leaders"
- "Define our [function] strategy"

### Avoid
- Tactical/execution-heavy descriptions
- Overemphasis on "fast-paced" or "hustle"
- Entry/mid-level framing
- Vague leadership without scope clarity

### Platform Considerations
- **LinkedIn**: InMail for passive outreach, detailed role scope
- **Google Ads**: Company brand + role type
- Emphasize company reputation and leadership team

---

## Executive/C-Suite (15+ Years Experience)

### Primary Motivations
- Legacy and impact
- Mission alignment
- Board/investor quality
- Compensation and wealth creation
- Team and culture building
- Strategic autonomy

### Messaging Priorities

| Priority | Why It Matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Company mission | Must believe in the vision |
| Strategic autonomy | Ability to shape direction |
| Team/culture | Building high-performing organizations |
| Equity/upside | Wealth creation opportunity |
| Board quality | Governance and support structure |

### Effective Language
- "Define the [function] vision"
- "Report directly to CEO/Board"
- "Build the team from the ground up"
- "Significant equity participation"
- "Transform the industry"
- "Trusted partner to [CEO/leadership]"

### Avoid
- Tactical job descriptions
- Overspecification of day-to-day tasks
- Entry/mid/senior framing
- Standard job posting language

### Platform Considerations
- **LinkedIn InMail**: Highly personalized, executive recruiter approach
- Executive networks, referrals often more effective than ads
- [VERIFY] Effectiveness of programmatic ads for executive recruitment

## Seniority Signals in Job Titles

Candidates search using seniority indicators. Include appropriate terms:

| Level | Title Signals |
|-------|---------------|
| Entry | Associate, Junior, Coordinator, Assistant, I/1 |
| Mid | Senior, Specialist, II/2, Lead (individual contributor) |
| Senior | Manager, Director, Principal, Lead (team), III/3 |
| Executive | VP, SVP, EVP, Chief, Head of, C-Suite |

[VERIFY] Current title conventions vary by industry - validate for creative, tech, and corporate functions.
