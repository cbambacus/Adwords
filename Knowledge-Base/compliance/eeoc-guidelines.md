# EEOC Guidelines for Job Advertisements

## Overview

The Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) enforces federal laws prohibiting employment discrimination. All job advertisements must comply with these requirements to avoid legal liability and ensure fair hiring practices.

---

## Protected Characteristics

Federal law prohibits discrimination based on:

| Protected Class | Governing Law |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Race | Title VII of Civil Rights Act |
| Color | Title VII |
| Religion | Title VII |
| Sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, sexual orientation) | Title VII |
| National Origin | Title VII |
| Age (40 and older) | Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA) |
| Disability | Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) |
| Genetic Information | Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA) |

## Prohibited Language in Job Ads

### Age-Related

**Never Use**:
- "Young"
- "Recent graduate" (implies age)
- "Digital native"
- "Energetic" (when implying youth)
- "Fresh out of college"
- "Maximum 10 years experience"
- "Looking for young professionals"
- "Join our young team"

**Acceptable Alternatives**:
- "Entry-level" (describes experience, not age)
- "Proficient with digital tools"
- "1-3 years of experience" (lower bound, not upper)
- "Early career professional"

### Gender-Related

**Never Use**:
- "Salesman" / "Saleswoman"
- "Waitress" / "Waiter"
- "He/she will..." (in job descriptions)
- "Chairman"
- "Manpower"
- "Guys" or "gals"

**Acceptable Alternatives**:
- "Salesperson" / "Sales representative"
- "Server"
- "They will..." or "The successful candidate will..."
- "Chairperson" / "Chair"
- "Workforce" / "Staff"
- "Team" / "Team members"

### National Origin / Citizenship

**Never Use**:
- "American only"
- "Must be U.S. citizen" (unless government requirement)
- "Native English speaker"
- "No foreign accents"

**Acceptable Alternatives**:
- "Must be authorized to work in the United States"
- "Fluent in English"
- "Strong English communication skills"

[VERIFY] Current I-9 and work authorization requirements.

### Religion-Related

**Never Use**:
- "Christian values"
- "Must work Sundays" (without flexibility language)
- Religious appearance requirements (unless BFOQ)

**Acceptable Alternatives**:
- "Schedule may include weekends; accommodations available"
- No mention of religion unless BFOQ applies

### Disability-Related

**Never Use**:
- "Must be able to stand for 8 hours" (unless essential)
- "Physical fitness required" (without justification)
- "No health problems"
- "Must have clean driving record" (without necessity)

**Acceptable Alternatives**:
- "With or without accommodation, must be able to [essential function]"
- Focus on essential job functions
- "Reasonable accommodations available"

## Bona Fide Occupational Qualifications (BFOQ)

In rare cases, characteristics that would normally be protected may be job requirements:

| Example | BFOQ Justification |
|---------|-------------------|
| Female attendant for women's locker room | Privacy requirements |
| Actor of specific ethnicity | Authenticity for role |
| Religious leader | Religious organization |

**Important**: BFOQ is narrowly applied. Age is almost never a BFOQ. Race is never a BFOQ.

## Essential Functions vs. Preferences

### Essential Functions
Must be able to be performed with or without reasonable accommodation:
- Core job duties
- Reason the position exists
- Limited number of employees available to perform

### Marginal Functions
Should not be used to screen out protected classes:
- Infrequently performed
- Not primary purpose of job
- Can be reassigned

**Example**:
- Essential: "Analyze financial data and prepare reports"
- Marginal: "Occasionally move boxes up to 20 lbs" (unless this is frequent)

## Writing Inclusive Job Descriptions

### Do's

1. **Focus on outcomes, not methods**
   - Instead of: "Must type 60 WPM"
   - Try: "Produce accurate written correspondence efficiently"

2. **Use gender-neutral language**
   - Use "they/them" or rephrase to avoid pronouns
   - Audit for gendered terms

3. **Separate requirements from preferences**
   - Clearly distinguish "required" from "preferred"
   - Question every requirement: Is this truly necessary?

4. **Include EEO statement**
   - "[Company] is an Equal Opportunity Employer"
   - Consider expanded statements

5. **Mention accommodations**
   - "Reasonable accommodations available for qualified individuals with disabilities"

### Don'ts

1. **Don't set unnecessary experience minimums**
   - "10+ years required" may exclude younger workers
   - Consider: "Significant experience" or "5+ years"

2. **Don't require degrees when experience suffices**
   - "Bachelor's degree or equivalent experience"

3. **Don't use coded language**
   - "Cultural fit" can mask bias
   - "Overqualified" often targets older workers

4. **Don't list physical requirements unless essential**
   - Only if truly necessary for the role

## Platform-Specific Compliance

### Google Ads
- Ad copy is short; ensure no discriminatory language
- Landing page must also comply
- Cannot target by protected characteristics

### LinkedIn
- Employment ads cannot exclude based on protected classes
- Cannot use age, gender, or other demographic targeting for jobs
- Profile targeting must avoid discrimination

### Indeed
- Job postings reviewed for compliance
- Cannot include discriminatory requirements
- Easy Apply questions cannot screen for protected characteristics

### Meta
- Special Ad Category: Employment
- Cannot target by age, gender, zip code
- Restricted audience targeting

## EEO Statements

### Basic Statement
"[Company] is an Equal Opportunity Employer. All qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, or veteran status."

### Enhanced Statement
"[Company] is committed to creating a diverse environment and is proud to be an equal opportunity employer. All qualified applicants will receive consideration for employment without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, disability, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by law. We actively encourage applications from people of all backgrounds."

### Accommodation Statement
"If you need assistance or an accommodation due to a disability, please contact [HR contact/email] to request accommodation."

## Red Flags in Job Ads

| Red Flag | Risk | Alternative |
|----------|------|-------------|
| "Young and energetic" | Age discrimination | "Motivated and enthusiastic" |
| "Native English speaker" | National origin | "Fluent English communication" |
| "Recent college graduate" | Age discrimination | "Entry-level / 0-2 years experience" |
| "Must be US citizen" | National origin (usually) | "Authorized to work in US" |
| "Clean-shaven" | Religious discrimination | Only if safety BFOQ |
| "No felony convictions" | May violate state laws | "Background check required" |
| "Physical stamina required" | Disability discrimination | Focus on essential functions |

## Complaint Process Awareness

Candidates can file EEOC complaints for discriminatory job advertisements. Process:
1. Charge filed with EEOC (180-300 days from incident)
2. EEOC investigation
3. Mediation offered
4. Finding issued
5. Potential litigation

**Prevention is key**: Review all job advertisements before publication.
