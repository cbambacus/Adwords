# Platform-Specific Advertising Policies

## Overview

Each advertising platform has specific policies governing employment advertisements. These policies complement (and sometimes exceed) legal requirements. Violations can result in ad rejection, account suspension, or permanent bans.

---

## Meta (Facebook/Instagram) - Special Ad Category

### Policy Overview

Meta requires all employment-related advertisements to be designated under the **Special Ad Category: Employment**.

**Documentation**: [VERIFY] Current Meta Business Help Center URL for employment ad policies.

### What Triggers Special Ad Category

Any ad that:
- Promotes job opportunities
- References hiring, recruiting, or employment
- Links to job listings or career pages
- Promotes job fairs or recruitment events
- Discusses workplace benefits as recruitment

### Targeting Restrictions

When Special Ad Category: Employment is selected, these options are **disabled**:

| Disabled Targeting | Reason |
|-------------------|--------|
| Age | Prevents age discrimination |
| Gender | Prevents sex discrimination |
| Zip code (precise) | Prevents redlining |
| Detailed demographics | Potential discrimination |
| Behaviors | Potential proxy discrimination |
| Lookalike Audiences | Standard version disabled |

### Available Targeting

| Still Available | Notes |
|-----------------|-------|
| Location | Minimum 15-mile radius |
| Interests | Limited set, vetted for compliance |
| Custom Audiences | From your own data |
| Special Ad Audiences | Alternative to Lookalikes |
| Connections | People connected to your page |

### Special Ad Audiences

Replacement for Lookalike Audiences:

- Based on Custom Audience source
- Uses different modeling approach
- May have different performance characteristics
- [VERIFY] Current Special Ad Audience effectiveness benchmarks

### Creative Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Discriminatory content | Prohibited (images, text, targeting) |
| Misleading claims | Prohibited |
| Clear employer identification | Required |
| Accurate job representation | Required |

### Ad Review Process

- Employment ads receive additional scrutiny
- Review may take longer than standard ads
- Rejections can be appealed
- [VERIFY] Current average review time for employment ads

### Common Rejection Reasons

1. **Missing Special Ad Category designation**
   - Fix: Select Employment in ad creation

2. **Discriminatory imagery**
   - Fix: Use diverse, representative images

3. **Age-related language**
   - Fix: Remove "young," "energetic," age references

4. **Exclusionary targeting**
   - Fix: Review and remove non-compliant targeting

5. **Misleading job information**
   - Fix: Ensure accuracy of all claims

### Penalties

| Violation Level | Consequence |
|-----------------|-------------|
| First violation | Ad rejected, must fix |
| Repeated violations | Ad account restriction |
| Serious/intentional | Account disabled |
| Pattern of abuse | Permanent ban |

## LinkedIn Advertising Policies

### Employment Ad Requirements

**Documentation**: [VERIFY] Current LinkedIn Marketing Solutions policy URL.

### Targeting Policies

LinkedIn has specific rules for employment advertising:

| Restriction | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Age exclusion | Cannot exclude age ranges |
| Gender exclusion | Cannot exclude genders |
| Protected characteristics | Cannot target/exclude |
| Member groups | Limited use for employment |

### Compliant Targeting

| Targeting Type | Compliance Status |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Job title | Compliant |
| Job function | Compliant |
| Skills | Compliant |
| Industry | Compliant |
| Company size | Compliant |
| Seniority | Compliant |
| Location | Compliant |
| Education (field) | Generally compliant |
| Age | NOT compliant for exclusion |

### Content Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Accurate job information | Title, description, requirements |
| Clear employer identity | Must represent actual employer |
| Non-discriminatory language | No protected class preferences |
| Legitimate positions | Must be real, open roles |

### InMail Policies

Additional rules for Message Ads:

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Sender | Must be real LinkedIn member |
| Content | Professional, relevant |
| Frequency | Capped per member |
| Opt-out | Must be honored |

### Job Slots Policies

For LinkedIn Jobs (non-Campaign Manager):

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Complete information | Full job details required |
| Easy Apply | Optional but must function |
| Timely responses | Expected but not enforced |
| Job validity | Must be currently open |

### Enforcement

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Policy violation | Ad rejected |
| Repeated issues | Campaign suspension |
| Serious violations | Account suspension |
| Terms violation | Legal action possible |

## Cross-Platform Compliance Checklist

### Before Publishing Any Job Ad

- [ ] **Targeting**: No exclusions based on protected characteristics
- [ ] **Language**: No discriminatory terms or preferences
- [ ] **Images**: Diverse, non-discriminatory representation
- [ ] **Accuracy**: All job information is current and accurate
- [ ] **Platform designation**: Special Ad Category selected (Meta)
- [ ] **Landing page**: Compliant and matches ad content
- [ ] **Employer ID**: Clear identification of hiring company
- [ ] **Legal requirements**: Salary transparency, EEO statement where required

### Regular Audit

- [ ] Review active ads monthly for policy compliance
- [ ] Update ads when policies change
- [ ] Document compliance procedures
- [ ] Train team on platform-specific requirements
